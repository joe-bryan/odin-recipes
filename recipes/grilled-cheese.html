Grilled Cheese

A grilled cheese sandwich being pulled apart by its cheese. Photo by Anthony Espinosa on Unsplash.

Description

Leave it alone. Usually, you say that when you don’t want to provoke something, like a bear. In this case, leaving it alone means the bread develops an incredible crunch that will make your grilled cheese stellar.

The kind of cheese used also plays an essential factor. It’s hard to beat American, but it lacks a lot of depth many other cheese have. For that, go with a medium to sharp cheddar.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 2 slices of American or cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 tbsp of butter
  • 1/2 tsp of dijon mustard
  • Kosher salt
  • Cracked black pepper

Steps

  1. Heat your pan to medium-high temperature. After two minutes, add the butter.
  2. After the butter has melted, put down both slices of bread on the pan. Now, we are going to leave alone for at least 3 minutes to develop color and texture. Keep an eye and nose out to make sure the bread is not burning.
  3. Add a slice of cheese to each bread slice. Watch as the cheese slowly melts.
  4. After one minute of the cheese melting, its time to turn off the pan and move the bread slices to a cutting board.
  5. Lightly add dijon mustard to the cheese slices and salt. Crack fresh black pepper as well.
  6. Stack the slices together and cut the sandwhich diagonally.
